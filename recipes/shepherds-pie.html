Shepherd's Pie

Description

Perfect for chilly, weather, the shepherd's pie is a staple in British cuisine. Served hot, it seeps comfort and warms the spirit. Often mistaken as a complicated dish, it is very simple to make. A mixture of minced meat, cheesey potatoes and sweetness from the carrots makes it a complete experience and of the best dishes to enjoy with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 5 carrots (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 3/4 cup beef broth

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and add cubed and peeled potatoes until tender for about 15 minutes
  2. Drain the potatoes and mix in the butter, the onion and 1/4 cup fo shredded cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste and set aside.
  3. In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook the carrots until tender but firm for 15 minutes.
  4. Drain the carrots.
  5. Heat a large frying pan and add cooking oil.
  6. Add onions into the frying pan and cook until clear.
  7. In the same frying pan, without removing the onions, add the ground beef or lamb and cooked until brown.
  8. Pour off excess fat and stir in flour. Cook for 1 minute.
  9. Add the ketchup and the broth into the frying pan. Bring it to a boil and reduce the heat to let simmer for 5 minutes.
  10. Take the ground beef mixture from the frying pan and spread it into a 2 quart casserole dish.
  11. Take the carrots and spread them on top of the meat in the casserole dish.
  12. Next, spread the mashed potatoes on top of the carrots until evenly distributed. Spread the rest of the shredded cheese on top.
  13. Bake in a preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until golden brown.
  14. Serve and enjoy!
